China ships 20% fewer magnets to the US despite the truce

Chinese rare-earth magnet exports to the US averaged 479 tons a month in the first half of 2026, about 20% below the pre-restriction norm, according to US customs data — a squeeze that lands squarely on the permanent-magnet motors inside EVs, factory automation and humanoid actuators. US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer calls Beijing's compliance "not perfect."
